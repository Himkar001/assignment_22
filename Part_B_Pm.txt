PART B — STRETCH PROBLEM

  ---------------------
  1. GROUPED ANALYSIS
  ---------------------

Task: Group data by Category and compute mean Purchase_Amount

Code: import pandas as pd

df = pd.read_csv(“data.csv”)

grouped = df.groupby(“Category”)[“Purchase_Amount”].mean()
print(grouped)

Output: Clothing ≈ 25000
Electronics ≈ 27000
Furniture ≈ 26000
Groceries ≈ 24000

Explanation: Data grouped by category and average purchase calculated.

  -------------
  2. BAR PLOT
  -------------

Code: import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

grouped.plot(kind=‘bar’)

plt.title(“Average Purchase Amount by Category”) plt.xlabel(“Category”)
plt.ylabel(“Average Purchase Amount”)

plt.show()

Output: Bar chart showing comparison of average purchase across
categories

Explanation: Helps identify highest and lowest performing categories.

  -------------------------
  3. NUMERICAL COMPARISON
  -------------------------

Features: Age vs Purchase_Amount

Code (Line Plot): df_sorted = df.sort_values(by=“Age”)

plt.plot(df_sorted[“Age”], df_sorted[“Purchase_Amount”])

plt.title(“Age vs Purchase Amount”) plt.xlabel(“Age”)
plt.ylabel(“Purchase Amount”)

plt.show()

Output: Line graph showing trend between age and purchase

Explanation: Shows how purchase amount varies with age.

  -------------
  4. INSIGHTS
  -------------

-   Electronics has highest average purchase
-   Groceries has lowest average purchase
-   No strong relation between age and purchase (random data)

  ------------
  CONCLUSION
  ------------

-   Grouping summarizes data effectively
-   Visualization helps understand patterns
